"These are mods that add alternative start dates, similar to those in CK3 and EU4. It’s as if your campaign had progressed historically from 1836 up to 1861, 1871, 1890, or 1919, with population, technology, and industrialization adjusted to match the chosen date"

[img]https://i.imgur.com/DCDufFL.png[/img]

This mod aims to provide a historically accurate reconstruction of borders and geopolitical balances over a given period.

Instead of focusing on a single year, this mod covers a 5 to 10-year timeframe, allowing the inclusion of all states, colonies, protectorates, and subjects that existed during that period.

The goal is to feel as close as possible to an official expansion, as if Paradox had introduced new starting dates in vanilla, with alternative map setups and configurations.

To achieve this, the mod significantly reworks:

[list]
[*] Country borders, adjusted to match historical realities
[*] Population, based on period-accurate demographic data
[*] Industrialization levels, adapted to the historical context
[*] Technology aligned with the starting date (e.g. in 1804, Tier 1 technologies for most countries and partial Tier 2 for France and Great Britain; in 1890 or 1919, a large portion of technologies already unlocked, as if the campaign had naturally progressed to that stage by the start date).
[*] The starting date (based on a precise year tied to a major historical event)
[*] Certain decisions and journal entries, disabled or adjusted when they become inconsistent with the timeframe
[/list]

The objective is to deliver an immersive, credible, and coherent experience, grounded in historical accuracy while remaining true to the spirit of the game.
